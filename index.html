Som Tam - Thai Green Papaya Salad

Som Tam is a spicy green papaya salad that's one of Thailand's most famous dishes. This refreshing and zesty salad combines the perfect balance of sweet, sour, salty, and spicy flavors.

Ingredients

Main Ingredients

  • 1 medium green papaya, shredded
  • 2 cups long beans, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/2 cup roasted peanuts

Dressing

  • 3-4 Thai chilies
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 2 tbsp palm sugar
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • 2 tbsp lime juice

Instructions

1

Peel and shred the green papaya using a julienne peeler or mandoline.

2

In a mortar and pestle, pound the garlic and chilies until crushed.

3

Add palm sugar, fish sauce, and lime juice. Mix well.

4

Combine the dressing with shredded papaya, long beans, and tomatoes.

5

Garnish with roasted peanuts and serve immediately.

Pro Tips

Spice Level

Adjust the number of Thai chilies to your preferred spice level.

Freshness

Serve immediately for the best texture and flavor.

Substitutions

If green papaya is not available, you can use green mango as a substitute.

Jasmine Rice with Prawns - Thai Sautéed Style

This fragrant Thai-style jasmine rice with prawns is a perfect balance of aromatic rice and succulent seafood. The dish is seasoned with traditional Thai flavors and can be prepared in under 30 minutes.

Ingredients

Main Ingredients

  • 2 cups jasmine rice, rinsed and drained
  • 3 cups water or chicken stock
  • 500g large prawns, peeled and deveined
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • 2 eggs, beaten

Seasonings

  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 thumb-sized piece of ginger, minced
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp white pepper
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 2 green onions, sliced
  • 1 red chili, sliced (optional)

Instructions

1

Cook the jasmine rice with water or chicken stock according to package instructions. Fluff with a fork and set aside to cool slightly.

2

Heat oil in a large wok or skillet over medium-high heat. Add garlic and ginger, and stir-fry for 30 seconds until fragrant.

3

Add the prawns and stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until they turn pink and are almost cooked through.

4

Push the prawns to one side of the wok and pour the beaten eggs into the empty space. Scramble the eggs until they're just set.

5

Add the cooked rice and peas to the wok. Stir-fry everything together for 2-3 minutes, breaking up any clumps of rice.

6

Season with fish sauce, soy sauce, and white pepper. Taste and adjust seasonings as needed.

7

Garnish with sliced green onions and red chili if desired. Serve hot.

Pro Tips

Rice Preparation

For best results, cook the rice a few hours ahead and let it cool completely before stir-frying.

Prawn Selection

Use fresh, high-quality prawns for the best flavor. If using frozen prawns, make sure they're completely thawed and patted dry.

Wok Technique

For authentic Thai-style stir-fry, use high heat and keep the ingredients moving constantly in the wok.
